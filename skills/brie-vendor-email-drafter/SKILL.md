# Vendor Email Drafter

## What It Does

Drafts a professional outreach email to an event vendor — such as a caterer, AV company, or venue — based on a short description of the event and what you need from them. Produces a polished, ready-to-send email with a clear subject line, friendly opening, specific ask, and next-step CTA.

## When To Use It

Use it when you need to reach out to a new vendor for the first time, request a quote, follow up on a proposal, or confirm logistics, and you want a professional tone without starting from a blank page.

## Inputs

- **Vendor type** — what kind of vendor (e.g. caterer, florist, AV company, shuttle service).
- **Event description** — a sentence or two: event type, approximate guest count, and date range.
- **The ask** — what you need from the vendor (e.g. quote, availability check, menu options, site visit).
- **Optional: sender name and title** — for the sign-off.
- **Optional: tone preference** — "formal" (default) or "warm and conversational".

## Output

A complete email draft with:

- **Subject line**
- **Greeting**
- **1–2 sentence event context**
- **Specific ask with any relevant details**
- **Proposed next step** (e.g. a call, a site visit, a written quote by a date)
- **Professional sign-off**

## Example Prompt

```text
Draft a vendor outreach email. Vendor type: catering company. Event: a 200-person awards dinner on a Saturday evening in March. Ask: request a sample menu and per-head pricing. Sender: Jordan Lee, Events Coordinator. Tone: warm and conversational.
```

## Safety Notes

- This is a fictional skill for GitHub practice. Do not use real vendor names, real event details, real company information, or real contact information.
- Use invented names and scenarios only.
- The skill drafts text only — it does not send emails or access any email system.
